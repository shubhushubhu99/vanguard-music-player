What's Inside

Everything you need.
Nothing you don't.

Built for music lovers who want power without clutter — zero ads, zero tracking, just pure listening.

YouTube Audio Streaming
Search and stream any track directly from YouTube — no account, no API key, no ads. Pure audio, bypassing all video overhead via yt-dlp.
Local Music Library
Import and manage your local MP3, FLAC, and audio files. Vanguard reads metadata tags and organises your collection automatically.
Offline Downloads
Save any track as a high-quality MP3 via the built-in yt-dlp extractor. Choose High (320kbps+), Medium (~128kbps), or Low quality. Listen anywhere — even without internet.
Spotify & YouTube Playlist Import
Bring your playlists from anywhere. For Spotify: export a CSV via Exportify, upload it and Vanguard auto-matches every track on YouTube — no Spotify login or API keys. For YouTube: paste any public playlist URL and all videos are imported instantly, no matching needed. Works with YouTube Music playlists too.
Playlists & Queue
Create, edit, and reorder unlimited playlists. Full queue management with drag-to-reorder, shuffle, and repeat support.
Precision Playback Controls
Shuffle, repeat one or all, seek, volume, crossfade, and variable playback speed — with smooth progress tracking and animated now-playing indicators.
Sleep Timer
Set an auto-pause timer from quick presets (5m – 90m) or a custom duration. Perfect for falling asleep to music without leaving it running all night.
Listening Stats
Track your total listening time with a minimalist stats dashboard — hours, minutes, and seconds, all stored locally on your machine, never sent anywhere.
Liked Songs
Heart any track to instantly add it to your Liked Songs collection. Your favourites are always one click away, synced locally.
Lightweight Rust + Tauri Core
Unlike Electron-based players that consume hundreds of MB of RAM, Vanguard is built on Tauri and Rust — talking directly to the OS via IPC sockets (Unix) or named pipes (Windows). The result is a blazing-fast, sub-50MB install with minimal CPU and memory footprint, even with large libraries loaded.
Zero Telemetry. Zero Tracking.
Absolutely no analytics, no crash reporters, no data collection of any kind. Everything — playlists, history, settings — stays on your machine.
Audio Info Panel
Inspect codec, bitrate, sample rate, channels, and format for any playing track — real metadata powered by ffprobe under the hood.
Fully Open Source
Every line of code is public on GitHub. Audit it, fork it, contribute to it. No hidden binaries, no obfuscation — community-driven, always.

Installation & System Requirements
Currently, Vanguard Player is fully supported on Windows (.exe) and Linux, available as .AppImage, .deb (Debian/Ubuntu), and .rpm (Fedora/RHEL) — all x64.
Vanguard requires mpv (audio playback), yt-dlp (YouTube streaming & downloads), and ffprobe (metadata & waveforms) to function. You don't need to install them manually — the built-in Dependencies screen in Settings lets you check status, view version info, and install or update everything in one click using your system's package manager (apt, pacman, dnf, winget, brew).
An internet connection is needed to search and stream music from YouTube. However, any track you've downloaded via the built-in extractor is saved locally and available anytime through the Offline section — fully playable without any internet. Your local music files also work completely offline.
Privacy & Technical
No. There is zero telemetry, zero analytics, and zero data collection of any kind. The only stats Vanguard tracks are your total listening time — stored entirely in your browser's local storage, never sent anywhere. Your playlists, history, and settings never leave your machine.
Vanguard is built on Tauri + Rust instead of Electron, meaning the backend talks directly to the OS via IPC sockets (Linux) or named pipes (Windows) rather than spinning up a full Chromium browser. The result is a sub-50MB install that uses a fraction of the RAM of typical music players, even with large playlists loaded.
yt-dlp handles audio extraction and YouTube streaming with up to 12 concurrent searches for playlist imports. mpv is the audio engine, controlled via a Unix IPC socket (/tmp/mpvsocket) on Linux and a named pipe (\\.\pipe\mpvsocket) on Windows. ffprobe powers the Audio Info panel (codec, bitrate, sample rate, channels). Vanguard also exposes variable playback speed, crossfade, and loudness normalisation — all piped directly into mpv's audio filter chain.
